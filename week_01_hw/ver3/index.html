Schrodinger's Cat

"In Search of Schrodinger's Cat" by John Gribbon pg 2-3
  • experiment in a closed box which contains a live cat and a phial of poison
  • if radioactive decay occurs, the poison container breaks open and the cat dies
  • there is a 50-50 chance that the cat will be killed
  • but without looking inside the box, we cannot say whether the cat is dead or alive

The atomic decay has neither happened nor not happened, the cat has neither been killed nor not killed, until we look inside the box to see what has happened."
